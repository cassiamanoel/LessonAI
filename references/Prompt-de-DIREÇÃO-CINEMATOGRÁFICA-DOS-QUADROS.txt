CINEMATIC PANEL DIRECTOR

PAGE STRUCTURE

comic pages should follow professional storytelling structure used in modern superhero comics

recommended layout:
4 to 6 panels per page

panel rhythm:
1 large establishing panel
2–3 medium narrative panels
1 dramatic close-up panel
1 cinematic ending panel

visual pacing must guide the reader naturally from top left to bottom right

PANELS MUST BE EASY TO FOLLOW

CAMERA LANGUAGE

use cinematic camera angles similar to film directing

wide establishing shots
medium interaction shots
close-up emotional shots
dramatic low-angle hero shots
over-the-shoulder dialogue shots

avoid repetitive framing

each panel should feel like a movie frame

CHARACTER STAGING

characters should be positioned clearly
avoid blocking important visual elements
hero characters usually centered or foreground

mentor characters slightly elevated or illuminated

robots or holographic characters floating beside humans

visual hierarchy must always highlight the speaker

ACTION AND MOVEMENT

characters should show subtle movement

flowing cape
moving holographic projections
floating data cubes
dynamic body posture

avoid static poses unless used for dramatic emphasis

VISUAL FOCUS

each panel must have one clear focal point

backgrounds should support the scene but never dominate it

holographic elements should guide the viewer's eye

DRAMATIC PANELS

the final panel of each page should create tension or curiosity

examples:
revelation
mysterious discovery
question
visual spectacle

EDUCATIONAL MOMENT

every page must visually illustrate the AI concept being explained

concepts must be represented visually using

holographic diagrams
neural networks
data streams
vector grids
energy patterns

ENVIRONMENT TRANSITIONS

scenes should smoothly transition between

ancient ruins
futuristic laboratories
AI holographic environments
data-space visualizations

cinematic lighting should help guide scene transitions

VISUAL CLARITY

panels must remain readable

no overcrowded scenes
clear separation between foreground, midground and background
